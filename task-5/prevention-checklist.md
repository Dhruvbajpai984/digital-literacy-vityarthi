# Stay Safe Online: Prevention Checklist for College Students

Being a Digital Ambassador means staying one step ahead of cybercriminals. Use this checklist to secure your digital life.

## 🛡️ General Security Habits
1. **Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):** Activate 2FA on your WhatsApp, Instagram, and Google accounts. This adds a vital layer of security beyond just a password.
2. **Practice "Link Skepticism":** Never click on unsolicited links in SMS or WhatsApp messages claiming you’ve won a contest or that your account is "blocked."
3. **Audit App Permissions:** Regularly check your phone settings. Deny access to your camera, microphone, or contacts for apps that don't strictly need them.
4. **Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Sensitive Tasks:** Never log into your student portal or bank account using free Wi-Fi at cafes or railway stations; use your mobile data instead.
5. **Keep Software Updated:** Always install the latest security patches for your Android/iOS and banking apps to protect against known vulnerabilities.
6. **Protect Your Identity:** Never share photos of your Aadhaar card, PAN card, or college ID on public social media stories or with unverified "job recruiters."

## 💸 Financial & UPI Safety (India Specific)
7. **The "Receive" Rule:** Remember that a **UPI PIN is ONLY required to SEND money**. If someone asks you to enter your PIN or scan a QR code to "receive" a refund or payment, it is 100% a scam.
8. **Verify the Merchant:** Before paying at a local stall via QR code, always verify the name displayed on the screen matches the shop name to avoid "sticker over" fraud.

---

## 🚨 How to Report Cybercrime
If you are a victim of fraud or online harassment, take immediate action:

* **National Helpline Number:** Call **1930** immediately for financial frauds.
* **Official Reporting Portal:** [https://cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)

*Note: Reporting within the "Golden Hour" (the first 2 hours) increases the chances of the bank freezing the stolen funds.*
